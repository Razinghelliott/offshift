Charleston Wedding Vendor Costs: A 2026 Breakdown

What the line items actually cost — photographer to florist to caterer — so you can build a wedding budget that survives contact with reality.

Charleston wedding budgets are a mix of precision and magic. Precision, because you can plan for them — these are real numbers with real ranges. Magic, because Charleston has a way of asking for one more votive, one more bouquet, one more round of shrimp-and-grits at midnight. Here's what 2026 pricing looks like for a mid-sized (100-130 guest) Charleston wedding, line by line.

The full picture

Most 120-guest Charleston weddings come in between $55,000 and $95,000 all-in, not counting rings, honeymoon, or attire. The range is big because venue choice is the biggest variable — a downtown historic house commands 2-3× what a barn on Johns Island does.

What moves the price

The date

Saturday in peak season (March-May, October-November) is 15-25% more than the same wedding on a Sunday or a weekend in July. If you're flexible on date, you'll save real money.

The guest count

Catering and bar scale linearly; most other vendors don't. Adding 20 guests to a 100-person wedding adds about $3,000-$5,000 in food/drink, but doesn't change your photographer or florist much.

The venue

Two weddings with identical everything else can differ by $20,000 based on venue alone. Downtown historic venues (Lowndes Grove, Charleston Library Society, William Aiken House) start at $14,000-$28,000. Plantations and barns usually run $8,000-$18,000. Backyard weddings save on venue but shift costs to rentals and tenting.

The service style

Plated service costs 20-40% more than stations or family-style. Family-style has become wildly popular in Charleston for good reason — it's social, visually striking, and less staff-intensive.

Where people over-spend

  • Florals. Easy to double your budget if you let a florist design without a ceiling. Set a number, share it, and pick one statement moment (a ceremony arch, a head-table runner) to invest in.
  • Cocktail complexity. A 5-cocktail signature menu looks ambitious on paper and creates 40-minute bar lines. Stick to two.
  • Overlapping coordinators. Some couples pay for a planner AND a day-of coordinator. One or the other is usually enough.
  • Rentals from out-of-town. Charleston has excellent rental houses (Snyder, Ooh! Events, Fields of Ambrosia). Out-of-town rentals add freight.

Where people under-spend (and regret it)

  • Photography. This is the thing you keep. A cheap photographer almost always reads as a cheap wedding in the photos. Most Charleston couples look back and wish they'd gone one tier up.
  • Bar staffing. Understaffing the bar is a mistake every wedding regrets by 8pm. Budget for enough hands.
  • Timeline buffer. Not a line item, but budget 45 minutes of "air" into your timeline. Charleston weather, trolley traffic, and one missing groomsman will find it.

A realistic 120-guest example

Saturday in October, downtown historic venue, family-style dinner, two signature cocktails, florals led by a statement arch, DJ (not band), full-service planner, 7-hour photography + 4-hour video:

  • Venue: $18,000
  • Catering ($185/pp × 120): $22,200
  • Bar (labor $2,100 + alcohol $3,800): $5,900
  • Photography: $6,500 · Videography: $5,500
  • Florals: $9,800 · Planner: $8,500
  • DJ: $2,600 · Officiant: $800
  • Hair + makeup: $1,600 · Invitations: $1,200
  • Rentals: $4,500 · Transportation: $1,400
  • Cake/dessert: $1,200

Approximate total: $89,700. Your mileage will vary. But if someone quotes you $180,000 for the "average Charleston wedding," they're probably selling you something.

FAQ

What's the average cost of a Charleston wedding?

$55,000-$95,000 for 120 guests. More for downtown historic venues and peak-season Saturdays.

What's the biggest line item?

Almost always catering, usually 35-45% of total budget once bar is included.

When should I book vendors?

Photographer, videographer, venue, and planner: 10-14 months out for peak season. Florist and DJ: 6-9 months. Hair/makeup, bartender, officiant: 3-6 months.
